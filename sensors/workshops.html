SMART SORTER ROBOT ARM

The Industrial Revolution was one of the greatest contributing factors to the modern age. Now, with the advancements of robotics, our industries are experiencing a major transformation, with robots replacing human labour for monotonous or high precision tasks. Robotic Manipulators have played a major role in this automation revolution, with multi-DoF arms performing delicate, precise, and monotonous tasks with high reliability. In this workshop, we will build a 3 DoF robotic arm. This arm will be capable of arranging objects placed haphazardly in front of it, into perfect order.

A camera on the top will detect the objects and send the object position data to the controller(Arduino). The controller sends appropriate commands to the arm, which then fetches each of these objects, and places them in the final desired position.

Content relevant to the workshop are:

1. Inverse Kinematics
2. Basics of Arduino
3. Image Processing(Outline)
4. Robot algorithm/code explanation.

Duration: 3 Hours

GESTURE CONTROLLED ROBOT

The objective of this workshop is to build a Gesture-Controlled differential drive robot. The robot is controlled by hand gestures. A mobile application is used to send the output of the accelerometer sensor in-built in the smartphone via Bluetooth communication using UART protocol to the microcontroller board, which is present on the differential drive robot. The microcontroller interprets the signals and controls the robot accordingly. Thus, a differential drive robot is controlled using hand gestures.

Concepts involved:

-> Microcontroller Peripheral Programming
-> Working and use of the Accelerometer Sensor
-> Analog to Digital Conversion
-> Bluetooth communication between Android Phone App and Microcontroller
-> Functioning of a differential drive robot

Duration: 3 Hours
